I am an undergraduate student and researcher at MIT broadly interested in AI/ML, particularly in applications of AI/ML in the life sciences. My work focuses on the intersection of computer science, neuroscience, chemistry, and biology.

I am studying Computer Science and Neuroscience (6-9) and Chemical-Biology (5-7) with a concentration in History (21H). My research explores computational approaches to understanding complex biological and cognitive systems.

I am broadly interested in consciousness, including the neurobiological basis of consciousness as well as artificially replicating consciosuness. I currently conduct research leveraging computational tools to understand how different environmental conditions affect the brain, but my research interests include statistical and deep learning methods for signal processing (with applications in neuroscience and biology), and deep learning for personalized medicine and drug discovery.
