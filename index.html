Computational neuroscience and machine learning for the life sciences.

BS candidate, MIT — Computer Science & Neuroscience (6-9), Chemical Biology (5-7)

mabdel03@mit.edu | CV | LinkedIn | GitHub | Scholar

I am an undergraduate student and researcher at MIT broadly interested in AI/ML, particularly in applications of AI/ML in the life sciences. My work focuses on the intersection of computer science, neuroscience, chemistry, and biology.

I am studying Computer Science and Neuroscience (6-9) and Chemical-Biology (5-7) with a concentration in History (21H). My research explores computational approaches to understanding complex biological and cognitive systems.

I am broadly interested in consciousness, including the neurobiological basis of consciousness as well as artificially replicating it. I currently conduct research leveraging computational tools to understand how different environmental conditions affect the brain, but my research interests include statistical and deep learning methods for signal processing (with applications in neuroscience and biology), and deep learning for personalized medicine and drug discovery.

Research

Computational neuroscience of environment & cognition

I'm investigating how environmental conditions shape neural dynamics, using computational tools to characterize population activity and behavior.

Statistical & deep learning for biological signal processing

I'm interested in models that recover structure from noisy biological time series — neural recordings, biosignals, and beyond — using methods at the intersection of statistics and modern deep learning.

Deep learning for personalized medicine and drug discovery

I want to understand how representation-learning approaches can drive individualized therapeutics and small-molecule discovery, with the long-term goal of building AI systems whose abilities mirror those of the human mind.
